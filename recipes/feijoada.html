Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is a traditional Brazilian dish with a rich history and a captivating flavor profile. Originating from the time of Portuguese colonization, feijoada has evolved into a beloved national dish. It is a hearty black bean stew made with various cuts of pork, such as sausages, bacon, and ribs, along with beef and sometimes smoked meats. Slow-cooked with onions, garlic, and a medley of spices, this dish showcases a harmonious blend of flavors—salty and savory from the meats, earthy and nutty from the beans, and subtly spiced with a hint of smokiness. Feijoada is typically served with rice, collard greens, orange slices, and a sprinkle of farofa (toasted cassava flour) to add texture. Its deep flavors and comforting nature make feijoada a delightful experience that reflects the cultural and culinary tapestry of Brazil.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of black beans
  • 500 grams of assorted pork cuts (sausages, bacon, ribs, etc.)
  • 250 grams of beef (such as stewing beef or beef brisket)
  • 1/2 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbs of vegetable oil
  • 1 tbs of smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp of ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Farofa (toasted cassava flour), for serving
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Collard greens, for serving
  • Orange slices, for serving

Steps

  1. Rinse the black beans under cold water and soak them overnight. Alternatively, you can use the quick-soak method by boiling the beans for 2 minutes, removing them from heat, and letting them sit for 1 hour before draining.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add the assorted pork cuts and beef to the pot, and cook until they start to brown, releasing their juices.
  4. Stir in the smoked paprika, ground cumin, bay leaves, salt, and black pepper. Mix well to coat the meats with the spices.
  5. Drain the soaked beans and add them to the pot. Pour enough water to cover the ingredients by a couple of inches.
  6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer, partially covered, for about 2 to 3 hours or until the beans are tender and the meats are cooked and tender.
  7. During the cooking process, check the liquid level occasionally and add more water if necessary to keep the stew from drying out.
  8. Once the beans and meats are cooked, taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
  9. Remove the bay leaves from the pot.
  10. Serve feijoada hot, accompanied by cooked rice, collard greens, orange slices, and farofa.
