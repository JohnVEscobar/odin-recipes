Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is a traditional Brazilian dish with a rich history and a captivating flavor profile. Originating from the time of Portuguese colonization, feijoada has evolved into a beloved national dish. It is a hearty black bean stew made with various cuts of pork, such as sausages, bacon, and ribs, along with beef and sometimes smoked meats. Slow-cooked with onions, garlic, and a medley of spices, this dish showcases a harmonious blend of flavorsâ€”salty and savory from the meats, earthy and nutty from the beans, and subtly spiced with a hint of smokiness. Feijoada is typically served with rice, collard greens, orange slices, and a sprinkle of farofa (toasted cassava flour) to add texture. Its deep flavors and comforting nature make feijoada a delightful experience that reflects the cultural and culinary tapestry of Brazil.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of black beans
  • 500 grams of assorted pork cuts (sausages, bacon, ribs, etc.)
  • 250 grams of beef (such as stewing beef or beef brisket)
  • 1/2 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbs of vegetable oil
  • 1 tbs of smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp of ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Farofa (toasted cassava flour), for serving
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Collard greens, for serving
  • Orange slices, for serving
