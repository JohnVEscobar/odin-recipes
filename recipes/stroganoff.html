Brazilian Stroganoff

Description

Brazilian stroganoff is a unique twist on the classic Russian dish, adapted to suit Brazilian taste preferences. Introduced in the mid-20th century, Brazilian stroganoff quickly gained popularity and became a beloved comfort food in the country. This variation typically features tender strips of beef cooked in a creamy sauce made with ketchup, mustard, onions, mushrooms, and sometimes heavy cream. The flavor profile is rich, tangy, and slightly sweet, combining the savory notes of the beef with the creamy and tangy elements of the sauce. It is commonly served over white rice and accompanied by crispy shoestring potatoes, creating a satisfying and flavorful combination. The Brazilian adaptation of stroganoff showcases the fusion of international influences with local ingredients and culinary preferences, resulting in a delicious and distinctive dish.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of beef steak, cut into thin strips
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 200 grams of mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon of Dijon mustard
  • 1 cup of heavy cream
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • Shoestring potatoes (optional), for serving

Steps

  1. In a large skillet or frying pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the beef strips to the pan and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the pan and set it aside.
  3. In the same pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and saut√© until the onion becomes translucent.
  4. Add the sliced mushrooms to the pan and cook until they are tender and any liquid they release evaporates.
  5. Return the beef to the pan with the mushrooms and onions. Stir in the ketchup and Dijon mustard, coating the ingredients evenly.
  6. Pour in the heavy cream and stir well to combine. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken slightly.
  8. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  9. Remove the pan from heat and sprinkle chopped fresh parsley over the stroganoff for garnish.
  10. Serve the Brazilian stroganoff hot over cooked white rice, and if desired, accompany it with crispy shoestring potatoes on the side.
